\section{Stacking two diatonic fourths with a third on top}

These are the "So What" family of voicings -- you'll recognize the sound almost immediately.

\chords{
\def\numfrets{6}
\chord{3}{x,x,p{2},p{2},p{2},p{1}}{}
\chord{5}{x,x,p{2},p{2},p{2},p{1}}{}
\chord{6}{x,x,p{3},p{3},p{3},p{1}}{}
\chord{8}{x,x,p{2},p{2},p{3},p{1}}{}
}

\chords{
\chord{10}{x,x,p{2},p{2},p{2},p{1}}{}
\chord{11}{x,x,p{3},p{3},p{3},p{1}}{}
\chord{13}{x,x,p{2},p{3},p{3},p{1}}{}
\chord{15}{x,x,p{2},p{2},p{2},p{1}}{}
}

Note that some of these are familiar.  One of them looks just like a G13 voicing.
